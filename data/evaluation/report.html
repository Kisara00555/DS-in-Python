📐 Evaluation Methodology

1. Context Relevance

Are the retrieved chunks actually relevant to the user's question? Detects retrieval failures and poor embedding quality.

2. Faithfulness

Is the generated answer grounded in the retrieved context? Detects hallucination — claims not supported by the source documents.

3. Answer Relevance

Does the answer directly address the question asked? Detects off-topic or evasive responses even when context was good.

4. Keyword Precision

Of the expected keywords in the ground truth, what fraction were found in the system's answer? Measures answer specificity.

5. Keyword Recall

What proportion of the expected ground-truth keywords were successfully reproduced in the system answer? Measures completeness.

6. F1 Score

Harmonic mean of Precision and Recall. Balances the trade-off between keyword coverage and specificity in a single number.

7. Cosine Similarity

Semantic similarity between the ground-truth answer and system answer, computed using the same embedding model (all-MiniLM-L6-v2) as the retrieval pipeline.
